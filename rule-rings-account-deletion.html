Rule Rings Data Deletion

Last Updated: July 10, 2026

Use this page to request deletion of analytics and advertising attribution data associated with your installation of Rule Rings.

Rule Rings does not use user accounts and does not store personal information on its own servers. Game progress (campaign state, daily streak, settings) is saved locally on your device.

1. Delete Your Local Game Data

To remove all data that lives on your device — campaign progress, daily streak, endless records, settings:

  • iOS: Settings → General → iPhone Storage → Rule Rings → Offload App (keeps icon, clears data) or Delete App.
  • Android: Settings → Apps → Rule Rings → Storage → Clear Data, or uninstall the app.

Uninstalling or clearing app data stops all future local data collection immediately.

2. Request Deletion of Analytics and Ad Attribution Data

Rule Rings uses Firebase Analytics (Google) and Meta (Facebook) install tracking. These providers may hold data linked to your device's advertising identifier (IDFA on iOS, GAID on Android).

To request deletion, send an email to:

mladenstojanovicpo@gmail.com

with the subject line Rule Rings Data Deletion Request and include:

  • Device platform (iOS or Android)
  • Approximate installation date, if known
  • Your device advertising identifier if you are willing to share it (found in your device privacy settings)

We will forward valid deletion requests to the relevant providers. You will receive confirmation within 30 days.

Direct provider requests: If you prefer, you can submit deletion requests directly:
  • Firebase / Google: Google Account data deletion tools
  • Meta (Facebook): Facebook data deletion page

3. What Gets Deleted

  • Firebase Analytics event records associated with your device identifier.
  • Meta (Facebook) install attribution data associated with your advertising identifier.

4. What May Be Retained

  • Aggregated, non-identifying analytics that cannot be linked back to a specific device.
  • App store billing records managed by Google/Apple (not controlled by Rule Rings).
  • Minimal logs retained for legal or security obligations where required by applicable law.
